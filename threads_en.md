# Poem Analysis — *Remember* (2026.02.26)

---

## Original Text

> Remember
>
> Everything has its season.
> Those who left were always meant to go
> do not blame yourself.
>
> When you become someone good,
> good people will find their way to you.
> Focus on yourself, and first become
> the person you wish to meet.
>
> even trials hardest to bear
> become, in time, the ground you grew from.
> The more you cling  to a person, a moment
>
> the further away it drifts.
> There is no eternal happiness,
> and no eter nal sorrow.

## Structure

- **Form**: Free verse, no rhyme scheme
- **Composition**: 3 stanzas + a 2-line concluding couplet
- **Tone**: Quiet yet resolute inner voice — words spoken to oneself

---

## Thematic Analysis

**Stanza 1 — Acceptance of Loss**
> *Everything has its season. / Those who left were always meant to go / do not blame yourself.*

Opens with the metaphor of seasons. A **providential acceptance** that every relationship and circumstance has its time. "Meant to go" is not fatalism — it is an invitation to release self-blame.

**Stanza 2 — The Paradox of Self-Development**
> *When you become someone good, good people will find their way to you.*

Not the law of attraction, but a **philosophy of inner growth first**. "The person you wish to meet" carries a double meaning — both an ideal other and an ideal self.

**Stanza 3 — Attachment and Impermanence**
> *The more you cling to a person, a moment / the further away it drifts.*

Deliberate use of enjambment. The break between "cling" and "drifts" **enacts the very sensation of losing grip**. Meaning is made through structure.

**Closing Couplet — The Middle Way**
> *There is no eternal happiness, and no eternal sorrow.*

Buddhist impermanence (無常) meets Stoic equanimity. A two-directional message: do not cling to joy any more than to grief.

---

## Stylistic Features

- **Declarative sentences** — assertion without question; an unshakeable voice
- **Concrete → Abstract** flow: people / seasons → happiness / sorrow
- **Minimalism** — each line stands complete on its own

---

## Suggestions for Revision

- `eter nal` → `eternal` (typo correction)
- "the further away it drifts" → `the more it drifts away` may feel more rhythmically natural
- A bridging image between stanzas 2 and 3 would smooth the transition

---

## Overall Assessment

Simple on the surface, profound underneath. It holds both self-consolation and consolation for others — without ever becoming preachy. The final two lines — *there is no eternal happiness, and no eternal sorrow* — leave the poem open: whatever the reader is living through, they can find themselves here.
