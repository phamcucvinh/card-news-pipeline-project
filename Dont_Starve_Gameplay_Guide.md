## Game Overview

Don't Starve is a survival game where you play as Wilson, a scientist trapped in a mysterious wilderness. Your goal is simple but challenging: **survive as long as possible**.

**Key Principle**: There is no "winning" - only surviving. Death is permanent (roguelike), and when you die, you start over from scratch.

---

## Core Game Mechanics

### The Three Vital Stats

**1. Health** ❤️
- Represents how much damage you can take
- Reaches 0 = Death or Resurrection (if you have resurrection items)
- Restore with: Food, healing items, sleeping

**2. Hunger** 🍖
- Constantly depletes over time
- Empty hunger = Continuous health loss
- Restore with: Any food item

**3. Sanity** 🧠
- Decreases from: Darkness, eating raw meat, fighting monsters
- Low sanity effects: Screen distortion, hostile shadow creatures spawn
- Restore with: Cooked foods, clothing items (top hats), picking flowers

### Day/Night Cycle
- **Daytime**: Safe for exploration and resource gathering
- **Dusk**: Shorter period before darkness
- **Night**: Extremely dangerous - Charlie (invisible monster) attacks in darkness
- Always have a light source at night (campfire, torch, etc.)

## Day 1: First Steps

### Immediate Priorities

**1. Gather Basic Resources** (First 30 minutes of daylight)
```
✓ Flint (for tools) - Pick up from ground
✓ Twigs (from saplings) - 12+ needed
✓ Cut Grass (from grass tufts) - 12+ needed
✓ Logs (chop trees with axe) - 10+ needed
✓ Food (berries, carrots) - 5 items maximum
```

**2. Craft Essential Tools**
- **Axe**: 1 Flint + 1 Twig (to chop trees)
- **Pickaxe**: 2 Flint + 2 Twigs (to mine rocks)

**3. Prepare for Night**
- **Campfire**: 2 Logs + 3 Grass (build before dark!)
- **Torches**: 2 Grass + 1 Twig each (for exploration at night)

**4. Cook Your Food**
- Place food items near fire to cook them
- Cooked food = Better hunger restoration + longer shelf life

### Critical Don'ts for Day 1
❌ Don't attack spiders, bees, or tallbirds
❌ Don't enter the marsh biome
❌ Don't build permanent structures yet (you'll relocate)
❌ Don't hoard too much food (it spoils)
❌ **DON'T STARVE!**

## Days 2-7: Early Game

### Main Objectives

**Day 2-3: Exploration & Scouting**
- Explore to find optimal base location
- Map out resources and biomes
- Look for roads (connect to important areas)
- **DON'T settle yet** - keep moving

**Day 4-5: Base Selection**

Choose location near:
- ✅ Road intersections (for easy travel)
- ✅ 2-3 rabbit holes (consistent food source)
- ✅ Ponds (frogs, fishing)
- ✅ Pig village or beefalo herd (1 screen away)
- ✅ Grass, saplings, and berry bushes

Avoid settling near:
- ❌ Walrus camps
- ❌ Sinkholes with bats
- ❌ Spider dens (too close)

**Day 6-7: Base Establishment**
```
Priority Crafting:
1. Science Machine (4 Logs + 4 Rocks + 1 Gold Nugget)
2. Backpack (4 Grass + 4 Twigs) - More inventory space
3. Log Suit (8 Logs + 2 Rope) - Basic armor
4. Spear (2 Twigs + 1 Rope + 1 Flint) - Weapon
```

### Resource Management Tips
- Keep 2-3 days of food in reserve
- Cook all meat before eating (avoid sanity loss)
- Don't waste gold early game (save for science machine)

## Days 8-20: Mid Game

### Infrastructure Development

**1. Science Machine Unlocks**
```
Essential Crafts:
- Backpack (inventory expansion)
- Shovel (dig up plants to relocate)
- Hammer (deconstruct structures, get pig skin)
- Fire Pit (permanent light source)
- Crock Pot (advanced cooking)
```

**2. Farm Setup**
- Dig up grass tufts with shovel
- Relocate to your base
- Fertilize with manure (from pigs/beefalo)
- Do the same for saplings and berry bushes

**3. Food Production**
- Set up 6-10 rabbit traps near rabbit holes
- Build drying racks for meat preservation
- Create farms with seeds (less efficient than foraging)

**4. Light Sources**
- **Lantern**: 2 Twigs + 2 Rope + 2 Light Bulbs - Portable light
- **Mining Hat**: 1 Gold + 1 Straw Hat - Hands-free light

### Exploration Goals
- Find Touch Stones (resurrection points)
- Locate wormholes (fast travel)
- Map all biomes
- Identify seasonal boss locations

## Day 21+: Winter Preparation

### Winter Survival Essentials

**Temperature Management**
```
Winter Gear:
- Thermal Stone (10 Rocks + 1 Pickaxe + 3 Flint)
  • Absorbs heat, releases slowly
  • Heat near fire before exploring

- Winter Hat (4 Beefalo Wool)
  • +120 insulation
  • Slows heat loss

- Puffy Vest (8 Beefalo Wool + 2 Beefalo Horn)
  • +240 insulation
  • Best winter clothing
```

**Food Stockpiling**
- Gather 40+ food items before winter
- Focus on crock pot recipes (multiply food value)
- Dry meat (lasts longer than fresh)

**Fuel Reserves**
- Stockpile 60+ logs
- Keep grass and twigs for torches
- Consider beefalo wool for fuel

### Winter Hunting: MacTusk
- Spawns in walrus camps during winter
- Drops valuable loot:
  - Tam o' Shanter (best winter hat)
  - Walking Cane (25% speed boost)
  - Blow Darts (ranged weapon)

## Combat Guide

### The B.A.R. Method

**Kiting Strategy** (Hit & Run)
```
1. BAIT: Let enemy attack first
2. AVOID: Dodge their attack
3. RETALIATE: Hit 2-3 times during cooldown
4. RETREAT: Back away, repeat
```

### Combat Difficulty by Enemy

**Easy** (Beginner-friendly)
- Spiders (1 hit pattern)
- Frogs (weak, slow)
- Gobblers (run away)

**Medium**
- Hounds (attack in packs)
- Tentacles (high damage, stationary)
- Treeguards (tank enemies)

**Hard** (Avoid until experienced)
- Clockwork enemies (knights, bishops, rooks)
- Giants (seasonal bosses)
- Shadow creatures (low sanity)

### Combat Equipment
```
Beginner:
- Log Suit (8 Logs + 2 Rope) - 80% damage reduction
- Spear (2 Twigs + 1 Rope + 1 Flint) - 34 damage

Advanced:
- Football Helmet (1 Pig Skin + 1 Rope) - 80% reduction
- Ham Bat (6 Meat + 2 Pig Skin + 2 Rope) - 59.5 damage
- Dark Sword (5 Nightmare Fuel + 1 Living Log) - 68 damage
```

## Food & Cooking

### Crock Pot Basics

**Benefits of Cooking**
- Multiplies food value (1+1+1+1 = better than eating raw)
- Removes negative effects (raw meat sanity penalty)
- Food lasts longer (6-20 days vs 2-4 days)

### Essential Recipes

**1. Meatballs** (⭐ Best early game)
- Recipe: 1 Meat (any size) + 3 Fillers (ice, berries, mushrooms)
- Result: +62.5 Hunger, +5 Health, +5 Sanity
- Lasts: 10 days

**2. Meaty Stew**
- Recipe: 3 Meat + 1 Filler
- Result: +150 Hunger, +12 Health, +5 Sanity
- Lasts: 10 days

**3. Pierogi** (⭐ Best healing)
- Recipe: 1 Meat + 1 Egg + 1 Vegetable + 1 Filler
- Result: +37.5 Hunger, +40 Health, +5 Sanity
- Lasts: 20 days

**4. Trail Mix** (Vegetarian option)
- Recipe: 1 Roasted Birchnut + 1 Berry + 1 Fruit + 1 Filler
- Result: +12.5 Hunger, +30 Health, +5 Sanity
- Lasts: 15 days

### Food Preservation Methods

**Drying Rack**
- Converts meat to jerky
- Jerky lasts 20 days (vs 6 days cooked meat)
- Restores sanity (+10-15)

**Ice Box**
- Slows food spoilage significantly
- Requires 2 Gold + 1 Gear + 1 Board
- Place near crock pot for efficiency

## Seasonal Survival

### Spring (Days ~21-35)
**Challenges**: Rain, wetness, lightning
- **Rain gear**: Umbrella, Rain Coat
- **Lightning rods**: Protect base from fires
- **Wetness effects**: Sanity loss, freezing, slippery items

### Summer (Days ~36-50)
**Challenges**: Overheating, wildfires, giants
- **Cooling items**: Ice Cube, Thermal Stone (cooled), Floral Shirt
- **Fire prevention**: Flingomatic (auto-extinguishes fires)
- **Ice source**: Ice Boxes, ice fishing in winter prep

### Autumn (Days ~51-70)
**Challenges**: Preparation for winter
- **Harvest season**: Gather maximum resources
- **Build reserves**: Food, fuel, materials
- **Easy season**: Similar to default starting season

### Winter (Days ~71-90+)
**Challenges**: Freezing, food scarcity
- **Stay warm**: Thermal stone, winter clothing
- **Food sources**: Koalefant hunting, ice fishing
- **Giants**: Deerclops (attacks on day 30.8+ of winter)

## Advanced Tips

### Character Selection
**Beginner-Friendly:**
- Wilson (default, grows beard for insulation)
- Willow (immune to fire damage, sanity from fire)
- WX-78 (gains stats from gears)

**Avoid Until Experienced:**
- Wolfgang (food penalty when mighty)
- Wigfrid (carnivore only, aggressive playstyle)
- Wes (hardest character - penalty to all stats)

### Resurrection Options
**Meat Effigy**
- Costs: 4 Pig Skin + 4 Beefalo Wool + 4 Beard Hair
- Resurrects at effigy location
- Permanent max health penalty (-30)

**Life-Giving Amulet**
- Costs: 3 Gold + 1 Red Gem + 2 Nightmare Fuel
- Wearable, resurrects on death
- No permanent penalties

**Touch Stone**
- Natural spawn in world
- One-time use resurrection point
- Find and activate before death

### Efficiency Tips
1. **Always cook food** - Better values, longer lasting
2. **Kite everything** - Avoid taking damage
3. **Base near resources** - Save travel time
4. **Use crock pot recipes** - Multiply food effectiveness
5. **Prepare for seasons** - Don't wait until last minute
6. **Learn spawn cycles** - Hounds every 3-13 days, giants seasonal

### Common Mistakes
❌ Building too early (wrong location)
❌ Not preparing for winter
❌ Fighting without armor
❌ Eating raw food (sanity loss)
❌ Exploring at night (waste of torch fuel)
❌ Staying in one place too long early game
